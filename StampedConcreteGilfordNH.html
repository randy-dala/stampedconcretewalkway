Case Study

Engineering a Lakefront Terrace on Varney Point

A Gilford homeowner on Varney Point wanted a grand lakefront entertaining terrace but faced three challenges: the NH Shoreland Water Quality Protection Act limited impervious surface coverage, the lot's steep grade toward the lake required retaining, and the sandy lakefront soil needed stabilization.

We designed a tiered terrace system that maximizes usable space within Shoreland limits by incorporating permeable concrete bands between stamped sections. A reinforced retaining wall stabilizes the grade, and a 16-inch engineered sub-base prevents settlement in the sandy soil. The upper terrace houses the outdoor bar and dining area; the lower terrace provides direct beach access. The homeowner now hosts their annual 4th of July party for 80+ guests — with fireworks reflected off the lake as the backdrop.

Gilford Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Gilford homeowners.

Are there special rules for lakefront patios in Gilford?

Yes. Properties within 250 feet of Lake Winnipesaukee are subject to the NH Shoreland Water Quality Protection Act, limiting impervious surface coverage. We're experts at maximizing your patio within these limits and handle all permitting.

Can you build on steep lake-to-house grades?

Absolutely! Steep lakefront grades are one of our specialties. We use terracing, reinforced retaining walls, and engineered drainage to create level outdoor living spaces on even the most challenging Gilford slopes.

What patterns suit lakefront properties?

Natural sandstone and travertine are our top picks for Gilford lakefront homes — their warm tones complement sandy beaches and lake colors. Flagstone works beautifully for mountain-side properties. We bring samples to your door.

Will the sandy lakefront soil cause settling?

Not with our engineering. We use deeper sub-bases (12-16 inches) with compacted crushed stone on sandy lakefront properties. This prevents settlement and frost heave, ensuring your patio stays level for decades.

Do you work on seasonal vacation homes?

Yes! Many Gilford lakefront homes are seasonal. We coordinate installation during your preferred timeframe and design patios that withstand both occupied and unoccupied seasonal conditions. Spring installations are popular for summer enjoyment.
