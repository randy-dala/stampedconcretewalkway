Case Study

Turning Ledge into a Design Feature on High Street

A Candia homeowner on High Street wanted a large backyard patio but their property had significant granite ledge just 8 inches below grade in several spots. The previous landscaper had recommended blasting the rock, which would have cost thousands and risked damage to the foundation.

Instead, we designed the patio to work with the ledge — leaving natural rock outcroppings exposed and framing them with the stamped concrete surface as intentional design elements. We adjusted the patio grade to flow around the ledge, created built-in planting pockets where rock met concrete, and selected a fieldstone pattern that harmonizes with the natural granite. The result is a unique, museum-quality outdoor space that cost 30% less than the blast-and-pour alternative.

Candia Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Candia homeowners.

Do I need a permit for a patio in Candia?

Candia generally does not require a building permit for standard ground-level patios. Properties near Tower Hill Pond or within wetland buffers may need Conservation Commission review. We verify all requirements during our free site assessment.

What do you do when you hit ledge during excavation?

Ledge is common in Candia, especially on hilltop properties. Rather than expensive blasting, we typically design around it — adjusting grades, incorporating rock as a design feature, or using alternative base preparation methods. Our approach is usually more cost-effective and creates truly unique results.

What patterns work best for Candia's country homes?

Fieldstone and flagstone are our most popular patterns in Candia, as they complement the town's rural character and natural surroundings. Cobblestone is another great choice for farmhouse entryways. We bring physical samples so you can see them against your home.

When is the best season for concrete work in Candia?

Our season runs late April through mid-October. Candia's higher elevation areas may hold frost a bit later in spring. Pondside properties benefit from waiting until late May for optimal soil conditions. Fall installations produce the strongest curing results.

Can you work around mature trees and landscaping?

Absolutely. Many Candia properties feature century-old trees and established gardens. We carefully plan access routes, install root barriers where needed, and protect all vegetation during construction. Our goal is zero impact on your existing landscape.
