Case Study

Transforming a Pleasant Street Victorian Backyard

A Concord couple renovating their 1890s Pleasant Street Victorian wanted an outdoor entertaining space that matched their home's grandeur, but the narrow, sloped backyard presented significant challenges. The existing space was a mix of crumbling asphalt, uneven flagstones, and exposed dirt.

We designed a multi-level patio system with European fan pattern stamped concrete, connected by gently graded walkways with integrated lighting. Retaining walls created level terraces on the slope, and a hidden drainage system channels water to the alley. The customers were so thrilled they hosted their daughter's wedding reception on the patio that same September — with 60 guests, a string quartet, and not a single complaint about the outdoor surface.

Concord Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Concord homeowners.

Do I need a permit for a patio in Concord?

Concord generally does not require a building permit for standard ground-level patios. Properties in the Merrimack River flood zone, downtown historic overlays, or near Horseshoe Pond may have additional requirements. We handle all code compliance during our free site visit.

Do you do both residential and commercial work in Concord?

Yes! As Concord's hometown contractor, we do residential patios, walkways, and pool decks as well as commercial entryways, restaurant patios, and office building approaches. Our commercial portfolio includes several downtown Main Street businesses.

What patterns are most popular in Concord?

European fan is our most popular pattern for Concord's Victorian homes, while ashlar slate dominates in the Heights and East Side. Fieldstone and flagstone are favorites for Penacook and West Concord properties. We bring samples to your door.

Can you match existing architectural details?

Absolutely. Concord's diverse architecture — Victorians, colonials, capes, craftsman bungalows — means every project is unique. We custom-match colors and select patterns that complement your home's specific era and style. It's one of the things we do best.

What about parking during installation?

We understand Concord's parking challenges, especially on Pleasant Street and downtown. We coordinate parking with neighbors, use compact equipment where possible, and always maintain pedestrian access. Our Chenell Drive crew staging means minimal impact on your street.
