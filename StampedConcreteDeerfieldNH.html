Case Study

Navigating Shoreland Protection on Pleasant Lake

A Deerfield homeowner wanted a large patio on their Pleasant Lake waterfront property, but the NH Shoreland Water Quality Protection Act imposed strict limits on impervious surfaces within 250 feet of the lake. Their original design exceeded the allowable coverage by 15%.

We redesigned the patio layout to maximize usable space within the regulatory limits, then worked with the Deerfield Conservation Commission to get approval. Key design innovations included permeable concrete bands between stamped sections that count as pervious surface, reducing the impervious footprint while maintaining a continuous aesthetic. The result is a 425 sq ft patio that meets all regulations, drains toward a rain garden, and provides exactly the lakeside entertaining space the homeowner envisioned.

Deerfield Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Deerfield homeowners.

Are there special rules for patios near Pleasant Lake?

Yes. Properties within 250 feet of Pleasant Lake are subject to the NH Shoreland Water Quality Protection Act, which limits impervious surface coverage. We're experienced at designing compliant patios that maximize your living space while meeting all requirements. We handle all permitting.

Do I need a permit for a patio in Deerfield?

Deerfield typically does not require a building permit for standard ground-level patios. Lakefront and wetland-adjacent properties may need Conservation Commission review. We check all requirements during our free site visit — just 20 minutes from our Concord office.

What patterns suit Deerfield's country character?

Flagstone and fieldstone are our most popular Deerfield patterns, as they blend naturally with the town's wooded lots and rural landscapes. Running bond brick is a favorite for historic colonials near the village center. We bring samples to your door.

What's the best time of year for concrete in Deerfield?

Our season runs late April through mid-October. Pleasant Lake properties should wait until late May when shoreline soils stabilize after snowmelt. South Road and upper-elevation properties can start as early as mid-April. Fall offers the best curing conditions.

How far is your office from Deerfield?

Our Concord headquarters is just 20 minutes from Deerfield center via Route 107. We're one of the closest premium concrete contractors to Deerfield — and we provide free on-site consultations. Call us and we can often schedule a visit the same week.
