Case Study

Taming a Steep Hillside on Montalona Road

A Dunbarton homeowner on Montalona Road had a beautiful hilltop view of the surrounding countryside but a back yard with a 25% grade that made it virtually unusable. Previous attempts to create flat areas with timber retaining walls had failed as the walls shifted in the freeze-thaw cycle.

We engineered a terraced patio system with stamped concrete retaining walls reinforced with geogrid fabric, creating three distinct level areas connected by decorative steps. The top terrace holds a dining area, the middle features a fire pit, and the lower terrace serves as a putting green pad. Hidden drainage channels route water safely downhill. Three winters in and not a single wall has moved a millimeter — even in Dunbarton's aggressive frost cycle.

Dunbarton Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Dunbarton homeowners.

Can you build on Dunbarton's steep, hilly terrain?

Absolutely! Steep grades are one of our specialties. We use terracing, retaining walls reinforced with geogrid, and custom drainage systems to create usable outdoor spaces on even the most challenging Dunbarton hillside lots.

How does Dunbarton's longer frost season affect concrete?

Dunbarton's elevation means the frost season can extend 2-3 weeks beyond towns like Concord. We adjust our concrete mix and curing techniques for these conditions and use deeper base preparations to prevent frost heave. Our Dunbarton installations consistently withstand the freeze-thaw cycle.

Do I need a permit for a patio in Dunbarton?

Dunbarton generally does not require a building permit for standard ground-level patios. Projects near Gorham Pond or identified wetlands may need Conservation Commission review. We verify all requirements during our free site assessment.

What patterns work best for Dunbarton's wooded properties?

Fieldstone and flagstone are our most popular patterns in Dunbarton, as they complement the town's heavily forested character. Hand-carved stone is another favorite for properties with a more rustic aesthetic. We bring samples to your door.

Can you access remote Dunbarton properties with your equipment?

Yes! Many Dunbarton properties have long, narrow driveways through the woods. We have compact equipment specifically for rural access and plan our logistics carefully. We've successfully completed projects on some of Dunbarton's most remote lots.
