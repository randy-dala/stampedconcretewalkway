Case Study

Solving River Road Drainage Challenges

A Bow homeowner on River Road wanted a large backyard patio for family gatherings, but their property sits just 200 feet from the Merrimack River. During spring runoff, the water table rises significantly, and the previous plain concrete slab had heaved and cracked within just four years of installation.

We removed the failed slab, installed a deep gravel base with integrated French drains, and poured a fiber-reinforced stamped concrete patio with expansion joints engineered for seasonal ground movement. The key innovation was a sub-surface drainage mat that keeps moisture from building up beneath the slab. Two years later — through two major spring floods — the patio remains perfectly level and crack-free.

Bow Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Bow homeowners.

Do I need a permit for a patio in Bow?

Bow generally does not require a building permit for ground-level patios. Properties within the Merrimack River floodplain or near wetlands may need review by the Bow Conservation Commission. We check all local requirements during our free site visit.

Will stamped concrete hold up near the Merrimack River?

Yes, with proper engineering. River Road and low-lying Bow properties require enhanced drainage systems beneath the slab to manage seasonal water table changes. We've perfected these techniques over decades of working along the Merrimack corridor. Our riverside installations regularly last 25+ years.

How close are you to Bow?

Our Concord headquarters is just 8 minutes from Bow center via I-93 or Route 3A. We're literally your closest premium concrete contractor — and we can be at your property the same day you call for a consultation.

What's the best time of year to install stamped concrete in Bow?

Our season runs late April through mid-October. River Road properties benefit from waiting until late May when spring runoff subsides. Upland Bow properties on Knox Road or Logging Hill can often start as early as mid-April when conditions allow.

What patterns are popular with Bow homeowners?

Ashlar slate and cobblestone are our most popular patterns in Bow, as they complement the town's New England character and wooded lots. Fieldstone is another favorite, especially for properties with existing stone walls. We bring samples to your door so you can see them in person.
