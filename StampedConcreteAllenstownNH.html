Case Study

Saving a Suncook Village Patio from Winter Damage

An Allenstown homeowner near the intersection of Main Street and River Road reached out after noticing surface flaking and discoloration on their 8-year-old stamped concrete patio. The cause? The patio was installed by another contractor without adequate drainage, and moisture from the nearby Suncook River was penetrating the surface and causing spalling during freeze-thaw cycles each winter.

Our team added a French drain along the patio's perimeter, repaired the damaged surface areas, applied a color-restoring antiquing wash, and sealed the entire patio with our premium two-coat acrylic sealer. The homeowner said it looked "better than the day it was first poured."

The takeaway: Moisture management is critical for any stamped concrete project near the Suncook River. We always engineer our Allenstown installations with proper grading and drainage — something not every contractor considers.

Allenstown Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Allenstown homeowners.

Do I need a building permit for a stamped concrete patio in Allenstown?

Allenstown generally does not require a building permit for a standard patio that is at ground level and not attached to the house. However, if your project involves a structure (like a covered pergola over the patio), is near the Suncook River floodplain, or falls within a wetland setback, you may need to check with the Allenstown Building Inspector's office on School Street. We handle this research for you during the estimation process.

Will stamped concrete hold up to Allenstown's river valley winters?

Absolutely. Allenstown sees an average of 60+ inches of snow per year, plus significant freeze-thaw cycling due to its location in the Suncook River valley. We prepare for this by using air-entrained concrete, installing a minimum 6-inch compacted gravel base (often 8–10 inches near the river), and applying a premium penetrating sealer that prevents moisture intrusion. When properly installed and resealed every 2–3 years, our stamped concrete easily lasts 25+ years in Allenstown's climate.

My property backs up to Bear Brook — are there any special considerations?

Yes, properties near Bear Brook or the Suncook River may fall within the town's Shoreland Protection zone or FEMA flood zone. This can affect how close to the waterway a patio can be installed and may require additional permitting. We're familiar with Allenstown's zoning regulations and will verify setback requirements for your specific lot during our free site assessment. We also engineer extra drainage precautions for riparian properties.

How long does a typical patio project take in Allenstown?

Most Allenstown patio installations are completed in 3–5 days depending on size and complexity. We typically pour in the morning and stamp in the afternoon, taking advantage of the east-facing slopes common in the Suncook River valley area that provide ideal curing conditions. Weather permitting, our season runs from late April through mid-October.

What's the best time of year to install stamped concrete in Allenstown?

Our peak season for Allenstown projects runs from late April through mid-October, when overnight temperatures consistently stay above 50°F. The Suncook River valley tends to hold moisture in the spring, so we often recommend waiting until May for properties close to the river to ensure optimal curing conditions. Fall is actually an ideal time — lower humidity and cooler temps help the concrete cure slowly and develop maximum strength before winter. We recommend booking early, as our Allenstown calendar fills up fast between June and August.
