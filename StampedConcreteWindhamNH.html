Case Study

From Crumbling Flagstone to a Showpiece on Castle Hill

A Castle Hill Road estate had an aging, frost-heaved natural flagstone patio that had become a tripping hazard. The homeowners loved the look of natural stone but were tired of the annual repair bills, shifted pavers, and weed growth between joints. They wanted the aesthetic without the maintenance headache.

We demolished the 1,200 sq ft flagstone installation, excavated down to stable soil, and engineered a commercial-grade sub-base with proper frost-depth footings. The new stamped concrete patio replicates the exact Pennsylvania bluestone pattern they loved — including the irregular edges and color variation — but as a single, jointless, weed-proof, frost-resistant surface. We matched the existing granite retaining wall colors and added integrated LED step lighting. The result is indistinguishable from natural stone but requires only biennial resealing. The homeowners now host 50-guest fundraising events on a patio that required $0 in repairs over three years — compared to $3,000+ annually for the old flagstone.

Windham Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions from Windham homeowners.

How does stamped concrete compare to natural flagstone?

Stamped concrete replicates the exact look of natural stone — including color variation and irregular edges — but eliminates weed growth, frost heaving, and annual repair costs. Our Castle Hill project proved it saves $3,000+ per year vs. natural flagstone maintenance.

What about lakefront regulations?

Cobbetts Pond, Canobie Lake, and Griffin Pond all have NH Shoreland Act restrictions. We design within impervious coverage limits, handle all DES permitting, and create spectacular waterfront installations that fully comply.

Can you handle estate-scale projects?

Absolutely! We've completed 1,500+ sq ft installations on Castle Hill and Range Road. We plan large-scale projects with phased pours and coordinate with landscape architects, pool builders, and general contractors.

What patterns complement contemporary architecture?

Italian slate in charcoal, travertine in ivory/cream, and geometric ashlar in cool gray tones. We match patterns to your home's architecture during the free consultation visit.

Do you coordinate with other contractors?

Yes! Windham's estate projects often involve multiple trades. We coordinate schedules and design elements with pool builders, landscape architects, electricians, and general contractors for seamless integration.
