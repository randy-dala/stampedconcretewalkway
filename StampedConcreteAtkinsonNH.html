Case Study

Engineering a Pool Deck Near Big Island Pond

An Atkinson homeowner on Island Pond Road wanted a stamped concrete pool deck but had been told by two other contractors that the high water table made the project too risky. Seasonal groundwater was reportedly surfacing within 18 inches of grade during spring thaw, causing concerns about heaving and cracking.

Our team performed a thorough site assessment and designed a raised-grade solution with a perimeter French drain system that redirects groundwater away from the deck surface. We poured on a reinforced base with additional fiber mesh for crack resistance and used a penetrating sealer rated for high-moisture environments. Three years later, the deck is still pristine — and the homeowner has since referred us to four of their neighbors along the pond.

Atkinson Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Atkinson homeowners.

Do I need a permit for a stamped concrete patio in Atkinson?

Atkinson typically does not require a building permit for a standard ground-level patio. However, properties near Big Island Pond or within identified wetland buffers may require review by the Atkinson Conservation Commission. We check all local requirements during our free site assessment so you never have to worry about compliance.

Will stamped concrete work with Atkinson's soil conditions?

Absolutely. Atkinson's glacial till soils generally drain well and provide a solid base for concrete. Properties closer to Big Island Pond with higher water tables may need additional drainage engineering — something we assess during our free site visit. With proper base preparation and sealing, our installations last 25+ years in New Hampshire conditions.

What patterns do Atkinson homeowners prefer?

Ashlar slate and fieldstone are our most popular patterns in Atkinson, as they complement the town's classic New England residential architecture. Herringbone brick is a top choice for walkways and entryways. We bring physical samples to your home so you can see exactly how each pattern looks against your exterior.

What's the best time of year for a concrete project in Atkinson?

Our season runs late April through mid-October. Southern NH towns like Atkinson often warm up a bit earlier in spring than communities further north, so we can sometimes begin as early as mid-April when conditions are right. Fall is ideal for curing — cooler temperatures and lower humidity produce stronger, more durable concrete.

How do you handle properties near Big Island Pond with high water tables?

Pondside properties require specialized drainage engineering. We typically install a perimeter French drain system, raise the grade slightly, and use fiber-reinforced concrete with a moisture-rated penetrating sealer. This approach has proven successful on numerous Big Island Pond properties over the years — even during the wettest spring thaws.
