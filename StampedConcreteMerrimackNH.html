Merrimack's Premier Stamped Concrete Contractor

Merrimack is a thriving Hillsborough County community of about 26,000 residents, strategically located between Nashua and Manchester along the Merrimack River. Known for the Anheuser-Busch Brewery, the Merrimack Premium Outlets, excellent schools, and abundant green space, Merrimack is one of southern New Hampshire's most desirable family towns.

We've completed projects throughout Merrimack — from the established homes along Turkey Hill Road, Bedford Road, and Baboosic Lake Road to the newer subdivisions near Continental Drive, Naticook Lake, and Wire Road. We also serve commercial properties along the Daniel Webster Highway corridor.

Merrimack's terrain includes Merrimack River valley floodplain, sandy glacial soils around Naticook and Baboosic Lakes, and till-covered hillsides. River valley properties need flood-resilient engineering, while lakeside homes require Shoreland compliance. Our southern NH experience covers every condition.

Merrimack Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions from Merrimack homeowners.

Do you work with builders and developers?

Yes! We partner with Merrimack-area builders for both individual homes and multi-home subdivision projects. Our coordinated palette approach gives each home a unique look while maintaining neighborhood cohesion.

What about properties near the Merrimack River?

River-adjacent properties require flood-resilient engineering. We use elevated sub-bases, perimeter drainage, and moisture barriers specifically designed for Merrimack River valley conditions.

Can you match different home styles in subdivisions?

Absolutely! We offer four-to-six pattern-color palettes that let homeowners choose unique looks while maintaining visual harmony with neighboring properties. Perfect for Merrimack's quality subdivisions.

Do I need HOA approval for a patio?

Some Merrimack subdivisions have HOA guidelines for hardscaping. We're experienced with local HOA requirements and can help prepare approval submissions with design drawings and material specifications.

How do you handle sandy lakeside soils?

Sandy soils near Naticook and Baboosic Lakes require deeper sub-bases (12-14 inches) with compacted crushed stone to prevent settling. Our engineering ensures stable, long-lasting installations on every soil type.
