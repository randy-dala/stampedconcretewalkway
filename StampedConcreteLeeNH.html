Lee's Go-To Stamped Concrete Contractor

Lee is a beautiful rural Strafford County community of about 4,500 residents, situated along the Lamprey River corridor and minutes from the University of New Hampshire in Durham. Known for its conservation lands, horse farms, and scenic country roads, Lee offers a peaceful New England lifestyle with easy access to the Seacoast and Route 4 corridor.

We've completed projects throughout Lee — from the hilltop properties along Lee Hill Road and Mast Road to the riverside homes near Wadleigh Falls, Tuttle Road, and Steppingstone Road. We also serve the newer developments near Route 155.

Lee's terrain features rolling glacial hills with sandy-loam topsoil over clay subsoil, scattered granite outcroppings, and riparian wetlands along the Lamprey River. The town's high water table in lower elevations and seasonal frost heave on hill slopes require careful sub-base engineering — our specialty.

The owner now drives tractors across the aprons daily with zero wear, and visiting clients compliment the professional appearance of the entire farm facility.

Lee Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions from Lee homeowners.

Can decorative concrete handle farm equipment?

Yes! We engineer heavy-duty aprons and pathways with reinforced mesh and thickened edges specifically designed for tractors, trailers, and daily equipment traffic. Our farm installations handle heavy loads year after year.

What about Lee's high water table areas?

Lower-elevation properties near the Lamprey River can have high seasonal water tables. We use engineered drainage layers and moisture barriers in our sub-bases to prevent frost heave and moisture-related damage.

Do you connect outbuildings to main homes?

Absolutely! House-to-barn, house-to-garage, and house-to-garden walkways are among our most popular Lee projects. We design for distance, grade changes, and year-round safety across the entire property.

What patterns suit Lee's rural character?

Fieldstone and irregular flagstone are natural fits for Lee's country properties. Pennsylvania bluestone complements historic farmhouses. We bring samples to your property for comparison.

Are there permit requirements in Lee?

Lee generally doesn't require permits for ground-level patios. Properties near the Lamprey River, protected wetlands, or conservation areas may need Conservation Commission review. We handle all verification.
