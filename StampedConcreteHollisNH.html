Case Study

Designing Around a Historic Stone Wall on Depot Road

A Hollis homeowner on Depot Road wanted a grand patio behind their 1840s farmhouse, but a beautiful 180-year-old dry-laid stone wall ran directly through the planned patio area. Removing it was out of the question — it's part of Hollis's agricultural heritage and adds irreplaceable character.

We designed the patio to incorporate the stone wall as a design feature rather than an obstacle. The stamped concrete flows up to and around the wall on both sides, with the wall serving as a natural divider between the dining terrace and the fire pit lounge. Custom color matching ensures the concrete blends with the wall's granite tones. The result is a patio that feels like it evolved organically over two centuries — exactly the aesthetic the homeowner envisioned.

Hollis Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Hollis homeowners.

Can you work around Hollis's historic stone walls?

Absolutely! Many Hollis properties feature heritage stone walls. We specialize in designing patios and walkways that incorporate existing stone walls as design features, color-matching concrete to complement the natural granite tones.

Do I need a permit for a patio in Hollis?

Hollis generally does not require a building permit for standard ground-level patios. Properties near Silver Lake, Flints Brook, or identified wetlands may need Conservation Commission review. We verify all requirements during our free site assessment.

What patterns complement Hollis's architectural style?

European fan and Pennsylvania bluestone are popular for Monument Square colonials, while fieldstone and irregular flagstone suit Hollis's country estates beautifully. We bring samples to your door for comparison.

Can you connect the main house to a barn or outbuilding?

Yes! This is one of our most popular Hollis requests. We design decorative walkways that elegantly connect main homes to barns, garages, and guest houses — common on Hollis's horse farms and larger properties.

What's the best season for concrete in Hollis?

Our season runs late April through mid-October. Hollis's sheltered valleys warm up slightly earlier than hilltop towns, making late April starts possible. Fall is ideal for curing and allows you to enjoy your patio by next spring.
