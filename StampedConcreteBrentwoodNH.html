Case Study

Conquering Clay Soils Near the Exeter River

A Brentwood homeowner on South Road had a frustrating history with outdoor hardscaping — their previous paver patio had settled unevenly within two years due to the heavy clay soils found in the Exeter River lowlands. They needed a permanent solution.

We excavated deeper than standard, removed the clay layer, and installed a compacted gravel base with geotextile fabric separation to prevent clay migration. The stamped concrete patio was poured with additional rebar reinforcement and control joints engineered for the soil's expansion characteristics. Four years later, the patio is dead level and the homeowner calls it "the only thing that's actually worked on this property."

Brentwood Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Brentwood homeowners.

Do I need a permit for a patio in Brentwood?

Brentwood typically does not require a building permit for ground-level patios. Properties within Exeter River setback zones or near identified wetlands may need Conservation Commission review. We verify all requirements during our free site assessment.

Will stamped concrete work with Brentwood's clay soils?

Yes, with proper engineering. The clay-heavy soils found in the Exeter River valley require deeper excavation, geotextile fabric, and enhanced base preparation. Our proven techniques have delivered lasting results on dozens of clay-soil properties throughout Rockingham County.

What patterns suit Brentwood's rural character?

Fieldstone and cobblestone are our most popular Brentwood patterns, as they complement the town's wooded lots and rural New England homes. Running bond brick is a favorite for colonials. We bring samples to your property for a perfect match.

When is the best season for concrete work in Brentwood?

Our season runs late April through mid-October. Properties near the Exeter River should wait until late May when soil moisture stabilizes. Upland Brentwood lots on Prescott Road and Pine Road can start as early as mid-April.

How do you protect existing landscaping during installation?

Brentwood properties often feature mature trees and established gardens. We use protective barriers, carefully plan access routes, and clean up daily. Our goal is to leave your landscaping exactly as we found it — with a stunning new patio or walkway as the only change.
