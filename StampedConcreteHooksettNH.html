Case Study

Flood-Proofing a Riverside Patio on Riverside Drive

A homeowner on Riverside Drive wanted a large entertaining patio but their property had flooded twice in the past decade during major Merrimack River events. Standard construction would risk repeated damage and costly repairs.

We engineered a flood-resilient patio system: an elevated reinforced slab on a deep-set crushed stone base designed to handle water flow without displacement. Stainless steel rebar and marine-grade sealer protect against moisture infiltration. The perimeter drainage channels direct flood water around the patio rather than under it. Two spring floods later — the patio emerged completely unscathed each time. The homeowner now enjoys their riverfront views without worrying about the next high-water event.

Hooksett Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions we hear from Hooksett homeowners.

Can stamped concrete handle Merrimack River flooding?

Yes! We engineer flood-resilient patios for Riverside Drive and other riverside properties using elevated slabs, marine-grade sealers, and channeled drainage systems. Our flood-proof designs have survived multiple high-water events.

Do I need a permit for a patio in Hooksett?

Hooksett typically does not require a building permit for ground-level patios. Properties near the Merrimack River or identified wetlands may need additional review. We verify all requirements at your free site assessment.

How quickly can you get to my Hooksett home?

Hooksett is just 10 minutes from our Concord headquarters! We can often schedule same-week site assessments for Hooksett homes, and our crews can be on-site within minutes if needed during a project.

What about the granite ledge on Granite Hill?

Granite ledge is common on Hooksett's higher-elevation properties. We have specialized equipment for ledge preparation and design foundations that work with — not against — the underlying rock. In many cases, we incorporate exposed ledge as a design feature.

What patterns are popular in Hooksett?

Ashlar slate is the top choice for Hooksett's newer subdivisions, while cobblestone and brick suit established neighborhoods. Flagstone is popular for properties with natural settings. We bring samples to compare in your actual outdoor lighting.
