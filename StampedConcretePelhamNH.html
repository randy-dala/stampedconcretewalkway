Pelham's Trusted Stamped Concrete Contractor

Pelham is a growing Hillsborough County community of about 14,000 residents on the Massachusetts border. Known for its beautiful Gumpas Pond, Long Pond, and proximity to Canobie Lake Park in neighboring Salem, Pelham combines affordable southern NH living with excellent schools and a strong community spirit.

We've completed projects throughout Pelham — from the established homes along Mammoth Road and Bridge Street to the growing neighborhoods on Sherburne Road, Marsh Road, Hobbs Road, and Windham Road. We also serve the lakefront homes around Gumpas Pond and Long Pond.

Pelham's terrain varies from flat sandy plains near the MA border to rolling wooded hills in the north. The town's mixed soils — from sandy loam to glacial till — require site-specific engineering. Many properties also have seasonal high water tables that need proper drainage solutions.

Case Study

Creating a Multi-Generation Backyard on Marsh Road

A Marsh Road family had three generations living on the same property — grandparents in the main house, parents in the addition, and kids everywhere. They wanted an outdoor space that served everyone: safe walkways for the grandparents, a play area for the kids, and an entertaining zone for the parents.

We designed a connected three-zone patio system: a covered ADA-accessible terrace off the grandparents' suite with gentle ramp and non-slip surfaces, a central entertainment patio with outdoor kitchen and fire pit for family gatherings, and a smooth play zone with basketball court apron and tricycle-friendly edges for the kids. Color-coded borders (warm bronze for the main patio, cool slate for the play area, and rose-toned for the accessible terrace) distinguish each zone while maintaining visual harmony. The multigenerational family now spends 80% of their summer evenings outdoors together.

Pelham Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions from Pelham homeowners.

Can you design kid-friendly outdoor spaces?

Absolutely! Pelham's family community loves kid-friendly designs. We create smooth play zones, basketball court aprons, tricycle-friendly edges, and non-slip surfaces that keep children safe while looking beautiful.

What about high water tables near ponds?

Properties near Gumpas and Long Pond often have elevated water tables. We engineer deeper crushed-stone sub-bases with perimeter French drains to manage moisture and prevent frost-heave damage.

Do you do ADA-accessible designs?

Yes! We design ADA-compliant terraces with gentle ramps, non-slip surfaces, and zero-threshold transitions — perfect for multigenerational homes and aging-in-place properties.

How does stamped concrete handle family traffic?

Our commercial-grade sealed surfaces handle bikes, scooters, furniture, and heavy foot traffic without cracking or wearing. The sealed finish makes cleanup from spills and BBQ grease easy too.

Can you connect multiple outdoor zones?

Yes! Multi-zone designs — entertainment, play, relaxation, gardening — connected by walkways are one of our specialties. Color-coded borders distinguish zones while maintaining visual cohesion.
