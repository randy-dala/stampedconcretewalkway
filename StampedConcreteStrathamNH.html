Stratham's Trusted Stamped Concrete Contractor

Stratham is an upscale Rockingham County seacoast community of about 7,500 residents renowned for Stratham Hill Park, the scenic Squamscott River tidal estuary, and the bustling Portsmouth Avenue commercial corridor. Located between Exeter and Portsmouth, Stratham combines rural New England charm with seacoast proximity and excellent schools.

We've completed projects throughout Stratham — from the estate homes on Winnicutt Road, Bunker Hill Avenue, and College Road to properties along Stratham Heights Road, High Street, and near the Squamscott River.

Stratham's terrain ranges from tidal-influenced clay near the Squamscott River to well-drained sandy loam on the uplands. The seacoast microclimate brings salt air, coastal humidity, and distinct freeze-thaw patterns that require seacoast-grade concrete engineering.

Case Study

Engineering a Squamscott Riverview Terrace

A homeowner along the Squamscott River wanted an outdoor entertaining space with tidal estuary views, but their property sat on heavy clay soils that expand and contract dramatically with moisture changes — and the salt air from Great Bay accelerates concrete deterioration.

We engineered a premium solution: 18 inches of compacted crushed stone sub-base with drainage fabric over the clay, marine-grade fiber-reinforced concrete, and a three-layer sealer system rated for salt-air exposure. The Italian slate patio features a curved overlook edge with custom iron railing overlooking the tidal marsh. We integrated heated walkway elements for ice-free access during winter. The homeowners now host monthly "tidal dinner parties" where guests watch Great Bay's famous tides from the terrace. Their property appraiser credited the installation with a significant value increase.

Stratham Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions from Stratham homeowners.

How do you handle salt-air deterioration?

Stratham's seacoast proximity brings salt air from Great Bay. We use marine-grade sealers, fiber-reinforced concrete, and three-layer protection systems designed specifically for coastal New Hampshire conditions.

What about clay soils near the Squamscott?

River-valley clay expands and contracts dramatically. We use deeper crushed-stone sub-bases (16-18 inches) with drainage fabric to isolate the concrete from clay movement and prevent seasonal shifting.

Can you match my formal colonial architecture?

Yes! Stratham's colonial and estate homes deserve equally refined outdoor spaces. Pennsylvania bluestone, antique brick, and formal herringbone patterns with granite accent borders complement any architectural style.

Do you offer heated walkway elements?

Yes! Radiant heating elements embedded in the concrete keep walkways and entryways ice-free all winter. It's a premium feature popular with Stratham's executive homeowners for safety and convenience.

How do you handle tidal-zone landscaping?

Properties near the Squamscott estuary require Shoreland Act compliance and tidal consideration. We engineer elevated foundations and proper drainage that accounts for tidal influence and salt-spray exposure.
