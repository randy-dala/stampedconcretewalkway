Case Study

Engineering an Oceanfront Terrace at Little Boar's Head

A Little Boar's Head estate owner wanted a grand entertainment terrace with unobstructed ocean views — but the property sits 30 feet above a rocky coastline, subject to salt spray, 100+ mph nor'easter winds, and FEMA coastal flood zone regulations. Previous concrete work had deteriorated from salt damage within five years.

We engineered a marine-grade system: a reinforced sub-base with stainless-steel rebar (not standard steel that corrodes), triple-applied marine sealers that repel salt penetration, and flex-joint systems that handle thermal cycling from ocean winds. We integrated low-profile wind walls on the seaward side and designed the terrace edge with safety railings that don't obstruct the panoramic Atlantic view. After five years, the surface shows zero salt damage — a first for the property. The homeowner says it's "the best investment we've made in 20 years of coastal living."

North Hampton Stamped Concrete FAQ

Common questions from seacoast homeowners.

How do you protect against salt air damage?

We use marine-grade triple-applied sealers, stainless-steel rebar, and salt-resistant concrete mixes specifically engineered for Atlantic coastal exposure. Our North Hampton installations show zero salt degradation after 5+ years.

What about FEMA flood zone compliance?

Many North Hampton oceanfront properties are in FEMA coastal flood zones. We design elevated foundations and breakaway-compatible structures that meet all federal and local flood regulations.

Can you build beach-access paths?

Yes! We design stepped coastal paths from house to beach with non-slip surfaces, proper grading, and dune-compatible construction that complies with coastal erosion regulations.

What patterns suit oceanfront properties?

Travertine in ivory/sandstone echoes the coastline. Weathered bluestone suits New England cottages. Flagstone in sea-gray tones complements rocky headlands. We bring samples for oceanside comparison.

How often should seacoast concrete be resealed?

Coastal properties should be resealed every 2-3 years instead of the standard 3-5 years due to salt exposure. We offer maintenance programs specifically for seacoast properties.
